What the fungus are grow box stacks?

A modular mushroom farm made from vertical stacks of polystyrene boxes.

Get in Touch


A sealed system that farms mushrooms vertically, allowing a greater output per square metre.


Step 1

Mycelium colonises the locally-sourced organic pine sawdust inside its grow box.

Step 2

Oxygen-rich warm air percolates through the stacked layers.

Step 3

We remotely drop the temperature and increase the humidity.



"The perfect micro-environment for converting the sawdust into wholesome delicious oyster mushrooms!"

The stack has three main parts


Base Stack

At the bottom of the stack is the air intake, heat source and humidity modifier. The fan is centrally controlled by the wifi temperature/humidity sensor. Custom user settings in Home Assistant can set up conditionals for different growth stages.

Mid Stack

The mid layers are mycelium inoculated sawdust. Fresh air blows through a series of ducts that link each layer of the stack. The moisture content of the air is controlled remotely through the Home Assistant. The boxes are kept at a constant temperature of 26.5Â°C

Top Stack

The moistiure content of warm air naturally rises while oxygen is fanned into the stack. This percolates through the stack and creates the perfect micro-environment for the mycelium to convert the sawdust into mushrooms.
